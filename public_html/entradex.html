The brief:
By the end of 2020, I chose to change jobs. Despite at time the world was still seeing the covid pandemic running rampants, I saw an interesting increase in people choosing to invest in stocks and the financial market. As a result, I accepted an employment offer from a Stock company in January 2021. I started researching and building Entrade X - Underlying Securities Web Platform in March 2021.

EntradeX carries the vision of a multi-platform that caters to two distinct groups of individuals - EntradeX web app for professional brokers, and EntradeX mobile app for end users.

Even though the EntradeX App was developed and successfully launched, Encapital still saw a very high demand with more advanced users, in particular those that tend to trade with higher value transactions and more complex trading experiences. The goal of the EntradeX Web App was to maximize profit in 2021 by increasing and attracting new customers through an web interface that caters toward more advanced traders.

Strategy

Company vision and mission

Vision: Becoming Vietnam's most valuable stocks firm.

Mission: Designing a simple trading platform that allows everyone in Vietnam to begin stock trading with ease.

Business model

EntradeX combines the B2C and Freemium business models.
B2C: Focus on retail clients to provide them with essential products.
Freemium: Provide more advanced products such as like margin loans or employing brokers, however, there are fees involved.

Board of director's expectations

  • The EntradeX App can solve the needs and flaws that the existing market is unable to solve.
  • In comparison to existing items on the market, the product must provide a positive customer experience.
  • In the two years since the product's release, the number of new users has increased by one million.
  • Reduce 3,3% the acquisition cost from 3.000.000đ to 100.000đ.
  • It's best to keep the internal IT team small (about 3-6 members per specific team).
  • Adhere to Agile methodology, which enables teams to deliver value to consumers faster and with fewer difficulties.
  • The CEO wants the entire client journey to be automated in the long run. From the initial approach to long-term client care and education on new features.

Product team setup

1 Product Owner, 3 Business Analysts, 2 Product Designers, 6 Front-End developers.

Design Process

Phase 1 - Ideation

1. Market Problem
  • When inflation rises and the economy does not recover as projected, bank's bad debts rise dramatically
  • Transparency and capital mobilization of listed companies.
  • The process of opening a securities account is lengthy, with numerous procedures and documentation to complete.
  • Vietnamese investors prioritize investment in real estate, gold and then the stock market
2. User Problem
  • Overwhelmed by information on the internet.
  • Is not aware of the finance market.
  • Because of the crash and reports of people losing all of their assets.
  • The user is skeptical about the stock market.
3. New Features or Enhancements

Entrade X is a product that performs similarly to other products in the market. However, the user experience has improved. On the other side, we are introducing a new Deal concept and Financial services. Additionally, automated all payments, pay debts flow, and other services to clients.

4. Market Research Report

As I did my market research to understand and determine if a new service was suitable in the current market, I adopted the mindset:
I accept that my product comes later than other big competitors in Vietnam such as VNDirect, SSI, TCBS, ..., however, I believe in the vision of my CEO and his strategies for this product to fit right into the current market.

As related to the Product Design aspect, I understand that doing research on my competitors is a must as well as it is even more important to understand the whys behind what makes them successful. Only through looking at the full Product from a big picture, then breaking down each part into smaller pieces and analyzing the strengths, weaknesses, targets, and status, ... can I then bring a better solution and improve the UI/UX experience for customers.

When I complete creating the blog on my website, I'll update the market research article.

5. User Interview

I had a clear goal while conducting a user interview session. I wanted to get as many insights as possible while keeping the conversations concise. It will save everyone time. Since brokers and premium clients are the first target audience for the web trading platform, I made a list of questions, designed a prototype, and took notes. Following the User interview sessions, I gather and analyze the information to create a report that I then provide to the Tech team.
Here are some insights I got from conversations:

  • It is easy to understand how the Broker and the Retailer behave differently.
  • During a trading hour, the spending time on the broker's screen outweighs the retailer's.
  • The Stock screener is prefered by Broker more than most.
  • 90% of trading customers like to use a dark theme more than a light theme. Because their eyes feel comfortable with the screen including a lot of information.
  • Brokers enjoy having a large "Order book" because they frequently need to quickly place, cancel, or copy multiple orders.
More wonderful ideas that I learned also helped me to further enhance my design…

Phase 3 - Development

1. UX Writing

As I have supported BAs from other projects with notification wordings, and taglines... and with experiences through multiple projects, I understand the crucial of content to users. Content is touching on users' feelings. With writing content, it is the small thing that matter such as the length of a sentence that should be adjusted to the screen size.

One of Entrade X's challenges is how to convey a long sentence in Vietnamese in short phrases in a field dominated by big data and numbers, yet the content should still bring a young, fast, and enthusiastic feeling to customers.

2. Design System

Despite the fact that Entrade X App had launched quite successfully and was met with many great reviews, it did not have any specific Design System that signified Entrade X.

As I joined the design team, I convinced the CEO that we really needed to build a consistent system for the product This Design System would not only be good for the product branding, but it will also be very useful to designers and developers as well. This alleviates any confusion between the design team and the development team as we are all on the same page when referring to any small elements within the app

3. Hi-fi Design

After the Product Owner confirms the flow. The Hi-Fi design will be uploaded to Zeplin. I will add comments to describe the details for developers.

4. Prototype Testing

I conducted UI and functional testing with early adopter users - mainly brokers - in the first phase. They were using the trading web MVP version, which had only the most basic functions: Login, logout, alter dark/light theme, track watchlist, deal book, portfolio, and buy/sell tickers are just a few examples.

I gathered user perceptions and expectations, as well as the challenges they face in the actual world. I discovered that the development team's thinking and end-user behaviour were vastly different.

As a result, while new features are being developed, I've chosen to ask and listen to end users more. Taking notes and organizing them in one space, such as the Notion platform, is also a fantastic idea. It makes it easier for me to keep track of and access information when I need it.

Phase 4 - Delivery

1. UAT test execution

Business testing was handled by business analysts. The BAs handled the API testing and usability testing, UI testing, and functional testing will all be done by UI/UX design team. Brokers, customer support representatives, and other staff also test and report any bugs.

I frequently share end-user comments or my point of view with BA and brokers for discussion. It's vital to encourage members to connect and share knowledge and opinion. Having team members actively inform each other about their current tasks and issues at hand is a wonderful way to connect members together

2. Measure Impact

The KPI

  • The biggest goal is to improve the registration procedure. How can users quickly start using Entrade X with a snap of their fingers?
  • Customers' satisfaction is measured.
  • Get insights from users' difficulties and concerns through real-life case studies every day.

The reports

The Entrade X App appears to be preferred by retail traders over the Trading website for placing trades. On this foundation, we will design and implement enhancements that will take us from a mobile app and then to a web-based trading platform.

On the other hand, the huge money flows from brokers' transactions are noticeable. Web trading should improve UX for Stock Screener and Order screener. Support brokers to Buy/Sell massive orders at a time.

Besides traditional marketing channels such as Facebook, Tiktok, Telegram, and Zalo, in order to attract new users, we also developed a Refer-a-Friend program. According to our data, the majority of new users were born in 2002. They are young, so having the opportunity to educate them about the stock and finance markets could be a good sign for us. But, because they are unfamiliar with the stock, the report shows that there is a risk that they will drop and forget about Entrade X.

To complete the registration procedure, users must complete three stages. EntradeX should improve this procedure to increase the number of users who complete the assignment because more than 80% of users do not want to spend time uploading their documents.

Conclusion

I had the honour of joining EntradeX Web Trading project at a very early stage. It was a wonderful opportunity to learn about what is new and interesting in the world of stock trading. As a Product Designer, I'm always looking for new methods and insights, and this project offered plenty of that.


6 things I learned from this project.

1. Planning ahead is a must-have with a Product Designer.

Always Prepare a long-term plan for any UX strategy. For me, much like user characters, a strategy is a fictitious-yet-realistic plan for Entrade X. I think the combination of Usability Testing and other frameworks, helps team members to be able to together make the guiding plan, which leads to us being on the same page when relying on customer feedback to keep building future features.

2. Tracking the progress of every single task.

I can see that work and time management has changed a lot since I pushed myself to log my tasks and keep track of the timeline for them. It is not only my to-do list but it's also helping me to think bigger. Why? Simply because I can see my work is better when I know what I am doing, why I am doing this and the correlation, the connection between all different issues throughout the design process.

3. Update constantly knowledge about business.

The Finance sector changes every single moment. The stock market is no different. Smaller space is my position as a Product Design for the Trading platform. Whether this is a requirement or not, I always make sure to be up-to-date with what's happening in my field, or I could lose credibility and potentially expose my product to risks. Understand the BA's requirements are good but I want to reverse advice to them also.

Even those my role as a Product Designer for a big Trading Platform is small, I always make sure to be up-to-date with what's happening in my field as I could potentially make a wrong decision that loses my product credibility or expose my product to unnecessary risks. Being able to adhere to BAs' requirements is good, but I also want to be able to give advice when needed as well.

4. Listening to feedback is good but a filter is more important.

How design feedback can go from bad to great with a few simple guidelines. Here are three signs that the feedback process is going wrong: review meetings going too long, discussions going off-topic, and no actionable solution included in the feedback. I think it is important not only to always include actionable solutions but these solutions should also to be based on business objectives and user needs.

5. A better investigation will save time for everyone.

Another lesson learned is to always invite users, and stakeholders to research meetings. Make sure to plan the research meeting and agenda 1-2 weeks ahead. This case study helped me to understand why more effort put into UX research is never a waste.

6. “Job to be done” helps the Product Designer and all teams address the right customer problems.

From my perspective, this term is the same as knowing yourself. This method helps me to think beyond the short-term factors of my product. The combination of empathy toward users and logical thinking - defines why a user uses the product and why that feature benefits for user and business.
